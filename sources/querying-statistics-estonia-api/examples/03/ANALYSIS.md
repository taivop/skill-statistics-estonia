# GDP per capita by county (Estonia, 2024)

## Executive summary
- The most recent county-level GDP per capita data (2024) show a clear lead for **Harju county**, driven by the capital region’s economic concentration.
- **Harju county** records 38,913.85 euros per capita, while **Valga county** records 14,792.02 euros per capita.
- The top-to-bottom ratio is **2.63×**, indicating substantial regional disparities in output per resident.
- The county median is **17,879.23 euros**, and the mean is **19,621.49 euros** per capita.

## Data sources and methodology
- **Primary data source:** Statistics Estonia API table **RAA0050: Gross domestic product by county (ESA 2010)**, indicator **“GDP per capita, euros”**, year **2024**.
- **Metadata reference:** ESMS metadata for RAA0050 (metadata ID 21406), which defines regional GDP (RGDP) as the output of resident economic units and documents the methodology and sources.
- **Methodology notes from ESMS:**
  - Regional GDP is compiled in line with **ESA 2010** and uses **NUTS** territorial classification and **EMTAK 2008 (NACE Rev. 2)** for activities.
  - Source data include administrative tax data (Tax and Customs Board), government accounts (Ministry of Finance), and other statistical activities such as national accounts, price indices, and structural business statistics.
- **Scope adjustments for this analysis:**
  - Included **only counties**; excluded “Whole country” and city subregions (“Tallinn”, “Tartu city”).
  - Values are **current prices** and expressed in **euros per capita**.

## Key findings
- **Highest GDP per capita:** Harju (38,913.85 euros).
- **Lowest GDP per capita:** Valga (14,792.02 euros).
- **Distribution:** Most counties cluster between roughly 17k–20k euros per capita, with Harju well above the rest.

## Conclusions and insights
- The 2024 county profile underscores a **strong capital-region effect**, with Harju notably above all other counties.
- Mid-ranked counties are tightly grouped, suggesting **moderate variation outside the capital region**.
- The bottom group (Valga, Põlva, Võru, Rapla, Jõgeva) indicates **persistent regional gaps** that are important for regional development policy.
